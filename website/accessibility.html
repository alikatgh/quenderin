Accessibility Statement

Last reviewed: 7 June 2026

We want Quenderin to be usable by everyone, including people who rely on assistive technologies. This statement covers this website.

Conformance

This website aims to conform to WCAG 2.1 level AA, the standard referenced by the European Accessibility Act (Directive (EU) 2019/882), the harmonised standard EN 301 549, the US Section 508, and the UK accessibility regulations.

Measures we take

  • Semantic HTML with a single, logical heading structure and landmark regions.
  • A "skip to content" link and fully keyboard-operable navigation and controls.
  • Visible keyboard focus indicators on every interactive element.
  • Text and interface colours chosen to meet AA contrast; meaning is never conveyed by colour alone.
  • Tabular figures and clear labels; the waitlist input has an associated label.
  • Reduced motion is honoured: with prefers-reduced-motion enabled, the animated gradient and scroll animations are disabled and a static frame is shown.
  • Decorative graphics (the gradient canvas and product mockups) are marked aria-hidden so screen readers skip them.
  • The site loads no third-party scripts, so it behaves predictably with assistive tech.

Known limitations

The hero uses an animated colour gradient behind white text; we apply a darkening layer and a text shadow for legibility, and the animation stops under reduced-motion settings. If you find any element hard to read or operate, please tell us — see below.

Feedback

If you encounter an accessibility barrier, contact us at albert.chromium@gmail.com and we will do our best to fix it promptly. Please include the page and a description of the problem.

Enforcement

If you are in the EU and are not satisfied with our response, you may contact your national accessibility enforcement body. In the UK, the Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) is responsible.
