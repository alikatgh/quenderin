# Quenderin — Facebook strategy

A practical plan for building an audience around Quenderin: **open-source LLMs
that run fully on your own phone or laptop — offline, no account, no telemetry.**
That identity — private, honest, engineer-built, genuinely free — is the whole
marketing advantage. Lean into it. Never sound like a hype account.

## Positioning & voice

- **What we are:** the model itself, living on your device. You pick a model that
  fits your hardware, download it once, and it runs offline through llama.cpp —
  on iPhone (App Store), Android (closed beta), and desktop (macOS/Linux, from
  GitHub). No cloud, no account, no data leaving the machine. MIT, every line
  public.
- **Voice:** plain, honest, technical when it earns it. We show real numbers, real
  code, and real limitations — the same voice as the website and the blog. Not
  salesy. A tool built by people who read the stack traces, not a startup pitch.
  Two rules, carried from the site:
  1. **No fake claims, ever.** A small local model is smaller than a datacenter
     model and *will* sometimes be confidently wrong. We say so, every time it's
     relevant. The honesty is the brand — it's why people trust the private part.
  2. **Every visual is real.** Screenshots are the actual shipping UI; numbers are
     measured (15 tok/s on an iPhone 12), not aspirational. Never mock up a result.

## The current growth push ⭐ (this is Quenderin's "campaign")

Two things just shipped, and they're the hook for the launch window:

- **iOS is live on the App Store** (free) — the headline. Lead with it.
- **Android is in closed beta** on Google Play — the recruitment drive. This is
  the equivalent of a targeted campaign: every few posts, invite testers (join
  the Google Group → opt in on Play). Google needs **12 testers engaged for 14
  days** to graduate the beta, so the honest "help us reach the finish line" ask
  is both true and a good community hook. Full flow: the beta guide post at
  `/blog-android-beta.html`.

## Content pillars (rotate these — one per weekday slot)

1. **Model & feature spotlight** ⭐ (Mondays, the anchor)
   One model or one capability, with a real screenshot: "Run Qwen 3 4B offline,"
   the fitness-aware model picker, the computer-use agent (macOS), offline mode,
   the model profile in plain language. Shows off what the app actually does. The
   screenshot is the ad — it's real UI, and that's rare organic credibility.
2. **Behind the engineering** (Wednesdays)
   Mirror the blog. Each engineering post is a Facebook post: the ggml `_exit(0)`
   crash, the model-integrity gate, how setup probes your hardware, the local
   computer-use agent. Honest war stories — including what each fix costs. This
   pillar is what makes a *developer* audience trust us.
3. **Honest reality / privacy / community** (Fridays)
   The real numbers page; "small models are sometimes wrong — here's how we grade
   them"; "works on a plane, in a tunnel, no account"; "read the code"; "what
   model should we add next?" This is the pillar that carries the values and
   invites replies.

Threaded through all three: the **Android-beta recruitment** posts and **iOS
launch** anchors, front-loaded into the first weeks.

## Cadence

- **3 posts / week** — Mon (spotlight) · Wed (engineering) · Fri (reality/community).
  Consistent-and-sustainable beats a burst then silence.
- The calendar runs **from the first full week through 31 Dec 2026** (~23 weeks,
  ~69 posts), generated by `scripts/gen-social-posts.cjs` into
  `docs/social/calendar.{json,md}`.
- Batch-scheduled: the first ~2 weeks are hand-placed in the Meta Business Suite
  Planner (fire via Facebook's own scheduler, no token); the rest are posted
  automatically by the GitHub Action once `FB_PAGE_TOKEN` is added. See
  `docs/social/FACEBOOK_SETUP.md`.

## Growth tactics

- **Link in the first comment, not the caption.** Facebook throttles posts with
  outbound links in the body; a link in the first comment ranks better. The
  poster does this automatically; for hand-placed posts, add the comment yourself.
- **The screenshot is the ad.** Post the real UI (the chat, the picker, the agent
  run log) — it's genuinely clean and it's honest. Don't post stock art.
- **Groups are the engine** for a niche like this: local-LLM / self-hosted-AI /
  privacy / Apple-silicon-ML / Android-power-user / open-source-AI communities.
  Share the spotlight and the engineering posts where they're on-topic, respect
  each group's rules, contribute before linking.
- **Boost the best organic performer**, not a cold post: let a spotlight run 48h,
  then put a small budget behind the highest-share one (interest: AI, privacy,
  open source; iOS/Android device targeting). $5–10 goes far.
- **Cross-post to Instagram** from the same Meta account — same visuals, near-zero
  extra effort, a different audience.
- **UTM-tag every link** so App Store / Play / GitHub clicks are provable.

## Metrics that matter (ignore vanity likes)

- **Shares** — the true signal; a shared post reaches a new network.
- **Link clicks** to the App Store / Play beta / GitHub (UTM-tagged) — the real
  conversion. For the beta window, **Google-Group joins and Play opt-ins** are the
  KPI (need 12 engaged testers × 14 days).
- **GitHub stars** and **"add model X" / "when Android public?" comments** — demand
  evidence. Prompt it: end reality posts with a real question.
- Review monthly; double down on the 2–3 post types that actually travel.

## Localization note (open decision)

Quenderin's first users are Russian-speaking, and the site ships a Russian
locale. Facebook content here is **English-primary** for now (the page bio, the
App Store, and the engineering audience are English), but the generator is built
to emit a **Russian stream** the same way Otter Master emits `--country` variants
— a fast-follow if we want to run a parallel RU calendar geo-targeted to
Russian-speaking regions. Flagged, not decided.
